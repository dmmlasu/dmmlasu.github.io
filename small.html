III: SMALL: Graph Contrastive Learning for Few-Shot Node Classification.

Description

The SMALL project investigates the potential of Graph Contrastive Learning (GCL) for Few-Shot Node Classification (FSNC) tasks across various supervision settings. This study emphasizes effective graph learning in situations with limited labeled data, focusing on robust methodologies that ensure accuracy in node classification under fully supervised, weakly supervised, and unsupervised settings.

Key areas of research include:

  • Benchmarking GCL and meta-learning methods for FSNC.
  • Developing methodologies for interpretability, robustness to adversarial attacks, and validation of mispredictions.
  • Collecting and curating datasets to enhance FSNC benchmarks.
  • Building a unified evaluation pipeline for standardized testing.
  • Releasing datasets and tools to support further research and community engagement.
